# PACE and EDA Approach in Data Analytics

## 1. Curiosity and Focus
Data analysts operate with a natural sense of curiosity; however, this curiosity must remain focused on the project's objectives. Balanced curiosity targets the specific problems the project aims to solve, rather than just chasing interesting but irrelevant narratives.

## 2. PACE Workflow
| Stage | Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Plan** | Understanding project goals and stakeholder expectations. |
| **Analyze** | Exploring, cleaning, and validating data. |
| **Create** | Structuring and transforming data into meaningful insights. |
| **Execute** | Presenting findings and sharing results with stakeholders. |

## 3. Integrating EDA with PACE
EDA (Exploratory Data Analysis) is applied throughout all stages of the PACE framework:
* **Discovery:** Aligned with the Planning stage.
* **Presentation:** Aligned with the Execution stage.
* **Applications:** Discovering, structuring, cleaning, joining, validating, and presenting.

## 4. Key Reminders
> **Ethics and Accurate Representation:** Data analysts are responsible for representing data accurately and ethically. Time constraints should never compromise the integrity of the data.

* **Focused Analysis:** Prevent confusion by working only with the necessary columns/features.
* **Communication:** Share the PACE plan with stakeholders to gather feedback and align priorities.
