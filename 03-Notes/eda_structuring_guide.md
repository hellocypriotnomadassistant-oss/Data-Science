# 📌 Structuring Data in Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

This guide covers the essential methods for organizing and preparing datasets for meaningful analysis and insights.

---

## 🎯 Objectives
* Make the dataset more understandable.
* Prepare data for advanced analysis.
* Extract meaningful **insights** from raw data.

---

## 🔑 Core Structuring Methods

### 1. Sorting
Arranging data in a specific order.
* **Example:** Ascending or descending order by value.

### 2. Extracting
Selecting specific columns of interest.
* **Example:** Selecting only "Tail Length" and "Pouch Volume".

### 3. Filtering
Choosing rows based on specific conditions.
* **Example:** Rows where tail length is less than 1 meter.

### 4. Slicing
Taking a specific portion of the dataset (rows and columns combined).
* **Formula:** Extracting + Filtering.

### 5. Grouping (Binning)
Categorizing data into meaningful segments.
* **Example:** Categorizing as Small, Medium, or Large.

### 6. Merging / Joining
Combining two datasets based on a common key (column).

---

## ⚠️ Critical Warning
**Do not change the meaning of the data!**
* Incorrect matching leads to incorrect analysis.
* **Accurate Data = Accurate Storytelling.**

---

## 🧠 The Workflow Logic
1. **Raw Data** → Structure & Organize
2. **Organized Data** → Perform Analysis
3. **Analysis** → Generate **Insights**

> **💡 Key Takeaway:** You cannot perform accurate analysis without properly structuring your data first.
