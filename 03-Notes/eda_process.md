# Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) & Hypothesis Generation

Taking short breaks and shifting your perspective during the **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)** process is essential for developing new insights. After the initial data exploration, a **hypothesis**—a preliminary, unproven explanation—is formulated to guide the direction of the analysis.

### Core Principles of the EDA Process:

* **Asking the Right Questions:** Precision in questioning is key to unlocking the true meaning of the data.
* **Decomposition:** Large, complex problems should be broken down into smaller, manageable sub-tasks.
* **Identifying Gaps:** Questions help reveal missing information and highlight what still needs to be discovered.

#### Practical Example:
For an airline company, you might investigate whether lowering ticket prices on specific days significantly increases passenger volume. You would then form a hypothesis based on this assumption and test it using the available data.

### Data Refinement & Transformation:
During the process, data is often manipulated to uncover deeper patterns:
* **Regrouping:** Changing data granularity (e.g., converting daily logs into monthly summaries).
* **Feature Engineering:** Creating new variables that provide better insights.
* **Formatting:** Standardizing units like currency or date/time formats.

### Main Objectives:
1.  **Storytelling:** Transforming raw data into a compelling and logical narrative.
2.  **Validation:** Reaching accurate conclusions by rigorously testing your hypotheses.

> **Pro Tip:** The emergence of more questions is a sign of progress, not failure. It indicates a deeper understanding of the dataset. Never hesitate to step back and refine your initial questions as the process evolves.
