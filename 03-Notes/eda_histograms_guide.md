# 📊 Understanding Histograms in EDA

Histograms are essential tools in Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) used to visualize the frequency distribution of a dataset.

---

## 🎯 Purpose of Histograms
* **Visualize Distribution**: Understand the shape of your data.
* **Identify Outliers**: Spot unusual or extreme values easily.
* **Locate Central Tendency**: See where the mean and median lie.
* **Guide Modeling**: Help decide which statistical tests or models to use.

---

## 📊 Common Histogram Shapes
1. **Symmetric (Normal Distribution)**: Data clusters around the center.
2. **Skewed**:
   - **Right-Skewed**: Long tail on the right side.
   - **Left-Skewed**: Long tail on the left side.
3. **Bimodal**: Two distinct peaks (suggests two different groups in data).
4. **Uniform**: Data is spread evenly across the range.



---

## 🐍 Implementation in Python
We primarily use two libraries:
* `matplotlib.pyplot.hist()`
* `seaborn.histplot()`

### Key Parameters:
* **`bins`**: Number of intervals.
* **`binwidth`**: The width of each interval.
* **`kde`**: Kernel Density Estimate (adds a smoothing line over the bars).

---

> **💡 Summary:** Histograms directly influence analysis decisions and model selection by revealing the underlying structure of the data.
