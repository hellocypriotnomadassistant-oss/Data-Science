# The Nature of EDA and Its Ethics

## 1. The Nature of EDA
EDA is **iterative and non-linear**; it does not follow a strict "step-by-step recipe." Since every dataset is unique, logic and experience dictate which applications are used, how often, and in what order.

## 2. The Six Core Applications
* **Discovering:** Examining the size, structure, and content of the dataset.
* **Joining:** Merging additional necessary data sources.
* **Validating:** Checking added or transformed data for errors and consistency.
* **Structuring:** Organizing data into different timeframes, segments, or formats.
* **Cleaning:** Addressing outliers, missing values, and data type conversions.
* **Presenting:** Sharing cleaned and visualized data with stakeholders or teammates.

## 3. Importance and Best Practices
EDA is critical for building ethical machine learning models:
* **Human Augmentation:** Keeps humans "in the loop" to reduce automated errors and biases.
* **Bias Evaluation:** Identifying and correcting imbalances or prejudices within the data.

> **Summary:** EDA must be flexible and tailored to the project goals. Ethical and accurate data representation is at the core of the exploratory process.
